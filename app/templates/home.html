FORMULA 1 REFERENCE GUIDE

WHAT IS FORMULA 1?


Formula 1, or F1, is team-based car racing that occurs all across the world. As of 2024, up to 20 drivers can participate in any given race. Historically, however, some races have had up to 34 drivers. Each of the teams have two cars they can potentially use in a race. An average F1 car can reach up to 370km/h or 229.91 mph!


WHAT IS A CONSTRUCTOR?


A Constructor refers to the various teams that Formula 1 racers are associated with. Though teams can be privately owned, it is not uncommon for an F1 team to be sponsored by a company. To be certified as a constructor, a prospective applicant must apply to the FIA (the governing body of F1) and pay a $200 million entry fee.


WHAT IS A CIRCUIT?


A Circuit in Formula 1 is a track where the races take place. As of August 2024, there have been 77 circuits used for different races. They are spread across 34 different countries throughout the world.
